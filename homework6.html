If $t_1$ is not the ancestor node of $t_0$, it is obvious that $\alpha_{k+1} > \alpha_k$
If $t_1$ is the ancestor node of $t_0$, let $t_{10}$ is the mark of node $t_1$ before pruning.
We have $\begin{cases} \frac{C(t_{10})-C(T_{t_{10}})}{\left\vert T_{t_{10}} \right\vert - 1} > \alpha_k \\ C(t_{10}) = C(t_1)\\ \left\vert T_{t_1} \right\vert = \left\vert T_{t_{10}} \right\vert - \left\vert T_{t_0} \right\vert +1 \\ C(T_{t_1}) = C(T_{t_{10}}) - C(T_{t_0}) + C(t_0) \end{cases}$.
Thus, $\alpha_{k+1} = \frac{C(t_1)-C(T(t_1))}{\left\vert T_{t_1} \right\vert -1} =\frac{C(t_{10})-C(T_{t_{10}})+C(T_{t_0})-C(t_0)}{\left\vert T_{t_{10}} \right\vert - \left\vert T_{t_0} \right\vert}>\frac{\alpha_k(\left\vert T_{t_{10}} \right\vert - 1)-\alpha_k(\left\vert T_{t_{0}} \right\vert - 1)}{\left\vert T_{t_{10}} \right\vert - \left\vert T_{t_0} \right\vert}=\alpha_k$.
So, $\alpha_{k+1} > \alpha_k$.
