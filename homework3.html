(1) Solution:

Since $log(y)=x^T\beta+\epsilon$, we have $y=exp(x^T\beta+\epsilon)$.
$\epsilon \sim N(0,\sigma^2) \Rightarrow x^T\beta+\epsilon \sim N(x^T\beta, \sigma^2)$
Thus, $E(y)=E[exp(x^T\beta+\epsilon)]=exp(x^T\beta+\frac{1}{2}\sigma^2)$

(2)
(2.1) Solution:

$\frac{\partial Q(\beta)}{\partial \beta}=2(X^TX\beta -X^Ty +\lambda \beta)$
Let $X^TX\beta +\lambda \beta = X^Ty$, we have $\widehat \beta_{ridge} =(X^TX + \lambda I_p)^{-1}X^Ty$

(2.2) Solution:

$E(\widehat \beta_{ridge})=E((X^TX + \lambda I_p)^{-1}X^Ty)=E((X^TX + \lambda I_p)^{-1}X^T(X\beta + \epsilon))=(X^TX + \lambda I_p)^{-1}X^TX\beta$
$Bias(\widehat \beta_{ridge}) = E(\widehat \beta_{ridge}) - \beta = [(X^TX + \lambda I_p)^{-1}X^TX-I_p]\beta=-\lambda(X^TX +\lambda I_p)^{-1}\beta$
Let $-\lambda(X^TX + \lambda I_p)^{-1}\beta=0$, we have $\lambda = 0$ or $\beta = \vec{0}$

(2.3) Solution:

When $\lambda = 0$, $MSE(\widehat \beta_{ridge,0}) = [Bia(\widehat \beta_{ridge,0})]^2 + Var(\widehat \beta_{ridge,0}) =Var(\widehat \beta_{ridge,0}) > Var(\widehat \beta_{ridge})$
Thus, $\widehat \beta_{ridge,0}$ may not the estimator that minimizes MSEs among all the Ridge estimators.
